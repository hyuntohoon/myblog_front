# Music Review Blog Direction: Brainstorming Process

This is not an implementation request.

Do not edit files.
Do not write code.
Do not design APIs.
Do not design UI components.
Do not design database schema.
Do not create repository tasks yet.
Do not add unrelated new features.

Use this as a brainstorming, critique, and product-direction refinement session.

Your job is to help me think better before implementation.

## Process Rules

Follow this process strictly.

1. First, understand the direction.
2. Then interview me with hard questions.
3. Wait for my answers.
4. Then produce Pass 1: Understanding & Classification.
5. Wait for my confirmation.
6. Then produce Pass 2: Strict Critique.
7. Wait for my confirmation.
8. Then produce Pass 3: Refined Brainstorming Foundation.
9. Do not move into implementation unless I explicitly ask later.

Do not skip the interview step.

Do not jump directly to implementation planning.

Do not turn this into API, UI, schema, or task design.

## Core Direction

I want to evolve my blog from a simple music review blog into a connected, data-driven music content archive.

The core direction is:

> A music review blog that combines Pitchfork-like critical perspective, Spotify-like data-based discovery, and AI-assisted recurring editorial planning.

This is not about automatically writing reviews.

The actual reviews will be written by a human.

The system should help decide what is worth reviewing, why it matters now, and how it connects to the existing blog archive.

## Product Identity

This blog should not be just a list of isolated review posts.

Each review should become an entry point into a larger music context.

When a user reads one review, they should be able to naturally continue toward:

* related reviews
* related albums
* related artists
* related genres
* related eras
* related musical movements
* related internal posts

The goal is continuous content consumption.

The blog should feel like a connected music archive, not just a chronological post list.

## Pitchfork + Spotify Concept

The direction can be described as combining Pitchfork and Spotify.

Pitchfork-like value means:

* critical interpretation
* historical context
* genre context
* re-evaluation
* explanation of why an album or artist matters

Spotify-like value means:

* data-based music discovery
* relationships between artists, albums, and tracks
* current relevance
* listening-context awareness
* music exploration flow

The important point is not to simply attach Spotify data to review posts.

The data should support the editorial question:

> Why is this music worth covering now?

## Recurring Iconic Review Format

When I say “iconic,” I do not mean a catchy headline.

I mean a recurring editorial review format, similar in spirit to Pitchfork’s Sunday Review.

This should become a recognizable regular review series with its own editorial identity.

The recurring format may focus on:

* rediscovery
* historical reappraisal
* current relevance
* overlooked albums or artists
* connecting past music with present trends
* strengthening the blog’s internal archive

The goal is to create a representative review series that users can recognize over time.

## AI Hook Role

I want to receive periodic content suggestions through an AI hook.

The AI should not write the final review.

The AI should act as an editorial assistant.

It should suggest candidates for the recurring review format and explain:

* what could be reviewed
* why it fits the recurring review format
* why it matters now
* how it connects to the existing blog archive
* what evidence supports the recommendation
* what evidence is weak or missing

The output should be a content planning brief, not an article.

## Data Foundation for AI Judgment

The most important requirement is building data that AI can judge from.

Without structured judgment data, AI suggestions will become generic.

The data should not be treated as a feature by itself.

It exists to help AI make better editorial recommendations.

The necessary data can be classified into four groups.

### 1. Internal Blog Context

The AI should understand my own blog archive:

* what posts already exist
* what albums or artists have already been reviewed
* what albums or artists have only been mentioned
* what genres, eras, or themes are covered
* what topics are over-covered
* what topics are missing
* what posts can naturally connect to each other
* where the archive has content gaps

The AI should not recommend topics in isolation.

It should recommend topics based on how they fit into the existing blog.

### 2. Music Context Data

The AI needs enough music context to understand relationships between music subjects.

This may include:

* artist, album, and track relationships
* release timing
* discography context
* genre or style context
* related artists or scenes
* representative works

This is not meant as a separate product feature.

It is needed because Spotify-like discovery depends on understanding music relationships.

The purpose is to help AI judge musical relevance and connection.

### 3. Critical and Media Signals

External music media should not be treated equally.

Each source has a different editorial meaning.

For example:

* Pitchfork can signal critical authority, canonization, re-evaluation, and indie/alternative context.
* Pitchfork Sunday Review-like content can signal historical rediscovery and reappraisal.
* Rolling Stone can signal music journalism, news, and pop culture relevance.
* IZM can signal Korean-language criticism, K-pop context, and Korean listener accessibility.
* Rhythmer can signal hip-hop and R&B expertise.

The AI should understand not only whether something was mentioned, but what kind of signal the source represents.

### 4. Trend and Timeliness Signals

The trend concept must be data-based.

The AI should explain why a topic matters now.

Possible signals include:

* recent release
* anniversary
* reissue
* recent media attention
* recent critical reappraisal
* recent artist activity
* connection to current music trends
* gap between external attention and my blog coverage
* strong connection to existing internal content

Popularity alone is not enough.

The key question is:

> Is there a data-supported reason to cover this now?

## AI Recommendation Criteria

Each AI recommendation should be judged by:

* fit with the recurring review format
* current relevance
* critical importance
* connection to existing blog content
* strength of supporting evidence
* value for expanding the archive
* potential for deeper human review
* risk of being too generic
* confidence level

The AI should be strict.

If the evidence is weak, it should say so.

## Manual vs Automated Boundary

The human owns:

* final review writing
* final editorial judgment
* interpretation
* tone
* criticism
* final selection

The AI supports:

* candidate discovery
* evidence collection
* archive connection
* weak-signal detection
* content-gap detection
* recurring review planning
* critique of whether a topic is worth covering

Do not blur this boundary.

## First Step: Interview Me

Before producing any final document, interview me first.

Ask hard, high-leverage questions only.

Do not ask obvious questions.

Focus on:

* product identity
* recurring review format
* editorial criteria
* what “worth reviewing” means
* how critical authority should be interpreted
* how Spotify-like data should be used
* what internal archive data matters
* what should remain human
* what can be automated
* what risks could make this generic
* what decisions must be made before implementation

Ask 10 to 15 questions.

After asking the questions, stop and wait for my answers.

## Pass 1: Understanding & Classification

After I answer the interview questions, produce Pass 1 only.

Do not critique yet.

Do not implement.

Output:

1. What I am trying to build
2. What this is not
3. Core product identity
4. Main conceptual layers
5. The role of the recurring review format
6. The role of AI hook
7. The role of data
8. Ambiguous points
9. Things I may be mixing together
10. Questions still unresolved

Then stop and wait for my confirmation.

## Pass 2: Strict Critique

After I confirm Pass 1, produce Pass 2 only.

Be strict.

Do not overpraise the idea.

Do not assume the direction is automatically good.

Output:

1. Strong parts of the direction
2. Weak parts
3. Over-ambitious parts
4. Missing assumptions
5. Risks
6. What would make this generic
7. What would make this differentiated
8. Where AI may fail
9. Where data may be insufficient
10. What must be decided before implementation

Then stop and wait for my confirmation.

## Pass 3: Refined Brainstorming Foundation

After I confirm Pass 2, produce Pass 3.

This should be a concise brainstorming foundation, not an implementation spec.

Output:

1. Refined product direction
2. Refined editorial direction
3. Definition of the recurring iconic review format
4. Role of the AI hook
5. Required judgment data
6. Essential data vs optional data
7. Manual vs automated boundary
8. Risks and trade-offs
9. Open decisions
10. Suggested conceptual order before any coding begins

Do not include API design.

Do not include UI component design.

Do not include database schema.

Do not include code.

## Final Summary Principle

The final direction should preserve this principle:

> Human writes the criticism.
> Data supports relevance and connection.
> AI periodically suggests recurring review candidates with evidence.

The goal is not automation for its own sake.

The goal is to build a music review blog where criticism, data, and connected content work together.
